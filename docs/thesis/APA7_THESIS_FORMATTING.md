# APA 7th Edition — Thesis Formatting Quick Guide

**For:** PINYA-PIC thesis figures, tables, timestamps, and references.

---

## Figures

- Place caption **below** the figure.
- Format: **Figure X.** *Title in sentence case.* Note source if adapted.

*Example:* Figure 4.1. *Positive mealybug detection heatmap by farm parcel (PINYA-PIC admin console, June 12, 2026).*

---

## Tables

- Place caption **above** the table.
- Format: **Table X.** *Title in sentence case.*

*Example:* Table 4.2. *Corrected test metrics for v16–v22 (locked protocol, June 12, 2026).*

- Number tables by chapter (Table 4.1, 4.2, …).
- Align with your school's list of tables.

---

## Dates and timestamps

Use **Month Day, Year** in prose and captions:

- Correct: June 12, 2026
- Avoid: 06/12/26, 12/06/2026

In tables, use the same format for `created_at` display columns.

---

## Metric tables

- Label **legacy vs corrected** test protocols separately.
- Do not mix training val metrics with corrected held-out test.
- Caption must state: images, instances, conf, IoU if reporting detection metrics.

---

## In-text citations

Author–date: (Author, Year) or Author (Year).

---

## References

APA 7th: author, year, title, source, DOI/URL. See sample block in `EXPLANATION_FOR_SIR_JUDE_MODEL_METRICS.md` §6.
